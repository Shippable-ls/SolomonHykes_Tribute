Summary of Solomon

Solomon Hykes was born in New York to an American father and a Franco-Canadian mother. His family came to settle in France, while four years 1 . After studying at the Epitech , which he released in 2006 (one of the very first promotions of this school), he began his career as an employee in a computer security company. In 2008, he created, along with another former student of Epitech, Sébastien Pahl, a SARL in Montrouge , named dotCloud . The creation of this activity is part of the emergence of the concept of Cloud Computing , and the company is initially positioned as one of the suppliers of PaaS 2 .

In 2010, the company dotCloud applied for the Y Combinator , a business incubator specializing in innovative projects on information and communication technologies . Solomon Hykes puts forward a concept, Docker , transposing to the software industry the idea of ​​the container that revolutionized the transport industry. The concept stems from a search for efficiency in software development and deployment, regardless of execution contexts. Their file is retained. In exchange for less than 10% of the capital of their company, dotCloud receives 20 000 dollars to realize the idea and to constitute a software base, based on the technological base developed in Montrouge. A small team is set up in Mountain View , to develop this software base. To prove the value of the product, they offer their technologies to other start-ups participating in the same Y Combinator program. They all need to rapidly implement their applications on infrastructures and make them evolve rapidly. Three months later, the crew boarded during the ritual Demo Day , having three minutes to convince the hundreds of investors present. One of the shocking arguments used is: "Look, your future start-ups have entrusted us with the stability of their applications! " 2 .
